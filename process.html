How it's made

Four crafts, broken into the steps you can still watch in a workshop.

01 / Textiles

Shawls, caps & carpets

  1. Fleece. Wool is washed in cold water, dried and sorted by length.
  2. Spin. Yarn is spun on a drop spindle or simple wheel through the quieter months.
  3. Weave or felt. Cloth goes on a pit loom in narrow strips; namda is soaked and rolled until fibres lock.
  4. Finish. Fringe is tied, embroidery or mirror work is added, and the piece is blocked flat.

See: textiles on the shelf

02 / Embroidery

Topis & chogah borders

  1. Cut. Cap crowns and coat panels are cut from firm cloth.
  2. Mark. Motifs are drawn lightly or counted from memory.
  3. Stitch. Cotton or silk thread fills geometric and floral bands; mirrors may be set into the work.
  4. Assemble. Sides are seamed, belts attached, and edges bound so the piece holds shape.

See: ladies cap, chogah

03 / Jewellery

Silver, stone & enamel

  1. Metal. Wire is drawn or sheet cut; pieces are shaped into bezels, bells and bangles.
  2. Set. Lapis, amethyst, sapphire or cabochons are seated and secured.
  3. Decorate. Filigree, granulation, oxidising or meenakari enamel adds colour and depth.
  4. Finish. Pearls or beads may fringe a jhumka; clasps and posts are checked before the piece leaves the bench.

See: jewellery on the shelf

04 / Bags & wallets

Jute, wool & needlepoint

  1. Weave. Accent bands are woven on a small loom; jute or wool forms the body.
  2. Stitch. Panels are seamed, lined and trimmed in contrasting cloth.
  3. Detail. Tassels, straps and embroidered faces are added by hand.
  4. Test. Straps and closures are pulled hard — these pieces are meant for market days.

See: bags & wallets
