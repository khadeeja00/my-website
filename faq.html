FAQ

Short answers for visitors and markers of this coursework site.

Is this a real shop?

No. Hunza Handicrafts is a student project. You can add pieces to a demo cart, but no payment is taken and nothing ships.

Who makes the products?

Each listing names a maker and a village. Read their stories on the artisans page, or open any product for materials and method.

Are the photos from Hunza?

The images on the shelf show handmade textiles, jewellery and bags in the style of northern Pakistan craft. Descriptions match what you see in each photo.

How long does a shawl or carpet take?

Weaving a shawl is often two to three weeks; a hand-knotted carpet can take months. Caps and bags are faster — a few days to a week. See making for step-by-step notes.

Can I visit workshops?

In a real shop, yes — by asking first. On this demo site, use the contact form as if you were booking a visit; the message is not sent anywhere.

What does the cart do?

It stores items in the browser for this session so you can practise the checkout flow. Totals reset when you close the tab.

Where is Karimabad?

In the Hunza Valley, Gilgit-Baltistan, Pakistan — uphill from Aliabad on the Karakoram Highway. The valleys page maps the villages we mention.

Why is there a history page?

Craft here grew from household need, fort geometry and trade routes. The history page ties that background to what sits on the shelf today.
