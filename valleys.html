The valleys

Six places along the river, and what each one makes.

From Aliabad up toward Passu, the Hunza river threads a string of villages. Craft follows climate and trade: wool where winters bite, embroidery where festival dress matters, silver where walkers pass the fort road.

Aliabad

Lower Hunza · co-op work

Market town and co-op hub. Bags, pressed goods and shared stalls leave from here for the highway.

Bags & co-op

Karimabad

Below Baltit Fort

Silver benches, stone setters and the demo shop on Baltit Road. Most jewellery on this site is tied here.

Silver & stone

Ganish

Old settlement · wool

Pit looms and felt work. Shawls and woven cloth for the shelf often start in Ganish winters.

Wool & weave

Altit

Beside the fort

Cloth coats and carved geometry that echoes the fort doorframes. The cream chogah on the shelf is from this side of the river.

Chogah & cloth

Gulmit

Upper Hunza · Gojal

Embroidery for caps and festival dress. Pakols and bright topis are finished in evening light after orchard work.

Caps & stitch

Passu

Toward the glaciers

Further up-valley, tribal skullcaps and mountain gear sit beside trekking routes. Patterns stay dense and colourful.

Tribal topi
